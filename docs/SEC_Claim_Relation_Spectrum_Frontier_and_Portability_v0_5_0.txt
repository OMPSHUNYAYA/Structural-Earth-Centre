STRUCTURAL EARTH CENTRE
CLAIM RELATION, CENTRE SPECTRUM, RESOLUTION FRONTIER, AND GRAPH-CENTRE PORTABILITY
Version 0.5.0

1. PURPOSE

This document specifies the retained advanced centre capabilities qualified in SEC v0.5.0.

The advanced centre layer does not replace the exact core, Earth profiles, real-land computation, Structural Centre Differential, Centre Stability Envelope, Symmetry Certificate, or Centre Resolution Certificate.

It provides four bounded capabilities:

- Structural Claim Relation;
- Centre Spectrum;
- Resolution Frontier;
- exact finite graph-centre resolution over a non-geographical carrier.

These capabilities remain centre-oriented.

The broader v0.5.0 cross-domain portability proof is specified separately in:

SEC_Structural_Portability_Proof_v0_5_0.txt

2. STRUCTURAL CLAIM RELATION

Two centre claims may differ without being unrelated.

The relation resolver compares:

- carrier identity;
- centre-profile identity;
- declared dependency maps;
- bounded result states and result material.

It can return:

CLAIM_EQUIVALENT
LEFT_REFINES_RIGHT
RIGHT_REFINES_LEFT
COMPATIBLE_OVERLAP
DECLARATION_CONFLICT
DISJOINT_DECLARATIONS

The accompanying result relation can be:

RESULT_EQUIVALENT
RESULT_DIVERGENT
UNRESOLVED_RESULT_RELATION

This separates:

claim relation != result relation

Two structurally different claims may converge to one result.

Two compatible claims may also produce different results.

A declaration conflict is not inferred merely from coordinate inequality.

3. CENTRE SPECTRUM

A family of centre claims should not be silently reduced to one average coordinate.

The Centre Spectrum preserves each member claim and groups the family by claim identity and result identity.

The bounded spectrum states are:

SINGLE_CLAIM_FAMILY
RESULT_CONVERGENT_CLAIM_DIVERSE
RESULT_DIVERGENT_FAMILY
MIXED_RESOLUTION_FAMILY
UNRESOLVED_FAMILY
INCOMPLETE
UNSUPPORTED

The governing policy is:

NO_BLIND_AVERAGING_OF_STRUCTURALLY_DISTINCT_RESULTS

A spectrum records:

- member count;
- resolved member count;
- distinct claim count;
- distinct result count;
- result-equivalence classes;
- pairwise structural claim relations.

The spectrum does not assert that all members are equally authoritative.

4. RESOLUTION FRONTIER

An unresolved claim may be missing structure or contain a declared conflict.

The Resolution Frontier asks:

What is the smallest declared repair set that would make this bounded claim admissible?

The current implementation evaluates only explicitly supplied repair options.

It can return:

ALREADY_ADMISSIBLE
UNIQUE_MINIMAL_FRONTIER
MULTIPLE_MINIMAL_FRONTIERS
NO_ADMISSIBLE_FRONTIER
UNSUPPORTED

The frontier does not invent missing evidence, choose an undeclared repair, or claim that the smallest structural repair is the best real-world action.

Its bounded principle is:

declared repair options + admissibility contract -> minimal structural frontier

5. EXACT GRAPH-CENTRE DEMONSTRATION

The advanced centre layer includes a synthetic exact finite graph-centre demonstration.

No additional external dataset is required.

The carrier is a finite unweighted undirected graph.

The current objective is:

MINIMAX_SHORTEST_PATH

For each node, the resolver computes exact shortest-path eccentricity.

It can return:

RESOLVED_POINT
MULTI_CENTRE
CONFLICT
INCOMPLETE
UNSUPPORTED

This demonstration shows centre-carrier portability:

same centre claim architecture + different carrier mathematics -> bounded centre resolution

It remains a centre problem and does not by itself establish cross-domain portability beyond centre claims.

6. RELATION TO THE v0.5.0 STRUCTURAL PORTABILITY PROOF

The advanced graph demonstration and the v0.5.0 portability proof have different boundaries.

Graph-centre demonstration:

centre architecture + non-geographical graph carrier -> centre result

Structural portability proof:

shared generic structural kernel + centre adapter -> generic structural semantics

shared generic structural kernel + non-centre admissibility adapter -> matched generic structural semantics

The v0.5.0 portability proof therefore tests a broader architectural separation:

generic structural semantics != domain adapter semantics

9. CLAIM BOUNDARY

The current advanced centre implementation does not establish:

- a universal algebra for every possible claim relation;
- causal proof from structural relation;
- optimal real-world repairs from a Resolution Frontier;
- authority equivalence among members of a Centre Spectrum;
- uncertainty coverage beyond explicitly declared families;
- new graph-theoretic centre mathematics;
- universal cross-domain portability;
- third-party verification.
