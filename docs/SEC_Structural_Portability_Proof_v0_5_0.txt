STRUCTURAL EARTH CENTRE
STRUCTURAL PORTABILITY PROOF
Release v0.5.0

1. PURPOSE

This document specifies the bounded Structural Portability Proof introduced in SEC v0.5.0.

The purpose is to test whether selected structural primitives remain semantically stable when the subject matter changes.

The proof does not claim universal portability.

Its bounded contract is:

same generic structural primitives + two declared domain adapters + frozen exact vectors
-> matched generic structural semantics and reproducible qualification evidence

3. SEPARATION CONTRACT

The portability architecture separates:

generic structural semantics

from:

domain-specific resolution semantics

The profile declares:

- domain adapters may resolve subject matter;
- the generic kernel contains no domain-specific resolution rules;
- relation, spectrum, frontier, and certificate semantics are shared.

This is tested directly by the portability audit.

13. CLAIM BOUNDARY

The current proof does not establish:

- universal portability across every domain;
- completeness of the generic structural primitives;
- semantic equivalence between all possible domain claims;
- causal inference from claim relations;
- production authority for the synthetic admissibility adapter;
- automatic execution authority;
- permission to invent undeclared repairs;
- third-party verification.

The strongest bounded claim is:

under the frozen v0.5.0 portability corpus, the same declared generic claim-relation, result-spectrum, resolution-frontier, and resolution-certificate semantics reproduce across two distinct bounded domain adapters, with independent Python/JavaScript parity.
